Ngati Raukawa

cultural ties and rights of the river

I am the River and the River is me

Ngati Raukawa named areas of their land with names, suggesting a cultural tie to the place. By 1945 stopbanks were implemented, confining the river and destroying philosophical and cultural ties
(Brierley et al., 2018).
